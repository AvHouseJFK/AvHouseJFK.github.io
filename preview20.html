Technical Operations

Runway Dynamics

KJFK operates with four runways in two crossing pairs: 4L/22R, 4R/22L, 13L/31R, and 13R/31L. 13R/31L stretches over 14,500 feet, making it the primary heavy departure artery.

The Canarsie Approach

A world-famous visual approach to Runway 13L. Pilots follow the Canarsie VOR (CRI) and perform a banking turn over Jamaica Bay at low altitude—a favorite for spotters at Howard Beach.

The JFK Legacy

1948: Idlewild Era

Opened as New York International Airport on former golf course land. Renamed in 1963 to honor JFK.

1970: The Jumbo Age

Pan Am launches the first Boeing 747 flight from JFK, changing global travel forever.

2026: The Mega-Hub

Ongoing $19B redevelopment including the New Terminal One and Terminal 6.
